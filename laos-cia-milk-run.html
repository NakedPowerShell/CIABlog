Laos CIA Milk Run

Milk Run, the 50 Kip

Air America (AA) flew regular scheduled flights to transport food, fresh milk, cargo, ammo, and people around Laos and Thailand.

The daily flights from Vientiane to Bangkok Thailand and back were such a common place event that it became known as the "Milk Run", as in going to the store to get milk. AA and CIA flippantly called it the 50 Kip, Kip being the local money and 50 Kip at the time was worth ten cents.



Laos did not produce good cows milk so it had to be daily imported from Bangkok. Even the Thai milk was not great compared to American dairies. [story]

The airplanes that AA used for the Milk Run were old WWII transport aircraft like the DC-3 and C-47 [story]. But they were always missing the side doors.



The term Milk Run was a bit of a classified term that was only used by the CIA people in the field. On a commercial flight (Royal Air Lao) out of Vientiane on our way out of the country, I accidentally said something about this flight will be true luxury compared to the 50 Kip flight.

My Mom turned white because I could have blown our cover, so she very discretely told me to be quiet. It was not until we got to Hawaii that I realized that the Milk Run / 50 Kip linkage would have been bad.
