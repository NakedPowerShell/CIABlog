Laos CIA Milk Run

Milk Run, the 50 Kip

Air America (AA) flew regular scheduled flights to transport food, fresh milk, cargo, ammo, and people (Diplomats, CIA, Sheep Dipped Soldiers, and Nurses) around Laos and Thailand.

The daily flights from Vientiane to Bangkok Thailand and back were such a common place event that it became known as the "Milk Run", as in going to the store to get milk. AA and CIA flippantly called it the 50 Kip, Kip being the local money and 50 Kip at the time was worth ten cents.



Laos did not produce good cows milk so it had to be daily imported from Bangkok. Even the Thai milk was not great compared to American dairies. [story]

The airplanes that AA used for the Milk Run were old WWII transport aircraft like the DC-3 and C-47. But they were always missing the side doors since the airplane was a work horse and did double duty dropping rice and cargo by parachute, so the doors would get in the way.

Air America: CIA Airline : Wikipedia

The aircraft Load Master aka Kicker (Guy that pushed cargo out the door with attached parachutes) needed a door free from obstacles. The aircraft also had a cord running down the top of the ceiling, to clip on the cargo packages to.

The term Milk Run and 50 Kip were a bit of an inside baseball terms that was only used by the CIA people in the field. On a commercial flight (Royal Air Lao) out of Vientiane on our way out of the country, I accidentally said something about this flight will be true luxury compared to the 50 Kip flight.

My Mom turned white because I could have blown our cover, so she very discretely told me to be quiet. It was not until we got to Hawaii that I realized that the Milk Run / 50 Kip linkage would have been bad.

50 Kip Flight = CIA

Not good.

I had to learn how to maintain cover and never reveal too much.

It was life and death for many people, not a video game that you could restart with no consequences.

This was a big burden for a 12 year old.
