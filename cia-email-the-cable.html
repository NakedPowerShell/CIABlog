CIA Email: The Cable

Secret Messages

Before email existed, the CIA used a communication system called a Cable.

The cable system was a secure method of typing email like letters and sending them to other classified cable users around the world.

The actual cable writing machine looked like a teletype machine. Except that the cable writing machine sent encrypted data so that the Russians, Chinese, and other hostile countries could not read our communications traffic.

 The format of a cable is the following:

* Classification: Unclassified, Classified, Secret, Top Secret, NOFORN, Special Keywords
* Priority: Regular, Priority, Immediate, Flash
* Tracking and Header Information
* Date and Time
* Subject Title
* Cable From
* Cable To
* Special Handling Notes
* Sources
* The Body of the Message
