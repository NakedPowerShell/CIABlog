Mekong River

Waterway of Life


Savannakhet is an extra sleepy little town that sits on the banks of the Mekong river, about midway down the left side of Laos.

 Taking a swim

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Savannakhet

The Mekong river is not a sanitary place to swim in, much less drink the river water or eat the catfish that are caught there. I believe that is an understatement.

The local Laotians do not understand the concept of cross-contamination of a water source that brings life to the whole ecosystem.

The Mekong is life for Laos, and transportation, food source, laundry mat, and dumping ground. As is so many things in Laos, it is a contradiction in itself. The dumping included human waste, trash, and dead bodies - human and animal.

So many NGO's (Non-Government Organizations) tried to educate and help the locals understand and deal with the consequences of their actions of not protecting the Mekong. 

During the Vietnam war and the Secret war, battles up stream could leave people and cargo to float downstream.

The Mekong is similar to the Mississippi river in that it is a long and very critical waterway. The Mekong upstream starts in the Tibetan Plateau then runs through China's Yunnan Province, Myanmar, Laos, Thailand, Cambodia, and Vietnam, with the terminal point the Mekong Delta in Vietnam.

So one day my Mom decided to take me and my Brother down to Savannakhet, to visit some of her friends. We jumped on the latest Air America Milk run plane [story] and flew down to Savannakhet.

The friends had a few kids my age and since it was very boring, and left to our own devices, we left the Savannakhet house and decided to go swimming in the Mekong. Mind you back at home in Vientiane, we would never have been let to do that, for safety and heath reasons.

So we get to the Mekong, on a typical hot sweaty humid day and waded out into the Mekong. We messed around for about two hours and then got hungry and tired of the sun, so we packed up and went back to the Savannakhet house.

My poor Mom was so upset when she discovered where we had been, she could hard speak. No worse for the wear, after a day of going to the movie theater and just being a sack of rice, we left and flew back to Vientiane by way of Pakse, which is a very traumatic story. [story]

We never spoke about the Mekong river swim again.

Years later when I learned the truth about the river, I know that me and my Brother got lucky that nothing happened to us.
