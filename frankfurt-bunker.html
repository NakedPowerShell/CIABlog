Frankfurt Bunker

WWII Bunker in Germany

When we moved to Germany we lived in two places. The first was a temporary situation at an apartment in downtown Frankfurt. The second place we lived for the longer term of the remaining two years was at an old German military apartment complex.

The complex was built before or during WWII and it had housed a lot of German soldiers.

It had an extensive underground tunnel system that connected each apartment building to a central bunker, in a spoke and hub configuration. We moved there in the early 60's and all the tunnels had been cemented over. The central bunker had a surface entrance in concrete that said "1944". The complex also had a long tunnel under the highway to a hospital far way.

I use to dig a lot in the dirt around the bunker hoping to find old German artifacts and I did find a 5 pfennig from 1944. This picture is a 10 pfennig.

During that time period my Father did a lot traveling, to other countries in Europe and to the Middle East.

One time he came home from a long business trip was wearing a beard, and me and my Brother hardly recognized him.

 

Who is that stranger?

He brought back many gifts from the Middle East, including two Keffiyehs and a Bible that had a hard wood book cover made from a tree in Jerusalem.


My Father stopped in Luxembourg on his return trip from the Middle East and visited someones house. I do not know if the house belonged to a friend, diplomat, or Agent but this person took this joke picture of my Father wearing one of the Keffiyehs. My Father made the joke "Lawrence of Luxembourg" instead of Lawrence of Arabia. 

Because of the family travels, I started collecting coins of foreign countries. My Father would never tell me where he had been doing CIA operations but he would come home and empty his pockets of his loose change into my collection. That was an OPSEC breach that he did on purpose to please me, to help my collection grow and indirectly tell me about his adventures.
