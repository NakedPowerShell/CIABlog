Dangerous Mango

Operation Mango: CIA mission


In Vientiane, the second house we lived in, it had a decent sized courtyard. [story]

I would ride my bicycle around the perimeter of the yard and I did not wear a shirt because of the heat that I generated in that high heat and humidity environment.

As I rode underneath a tree on the edge of the courtyard, the tree would scrape my back over and over again, as I ducked down going past it. I developed some kind of rash and my Mother took me in the dispensary to see the America doctor.

The doctor examined me and asked questions about my physical activities that could have contributed to this rash.

I told him about the tree and he asked my Mother what kind of tree it was. She said it was a mango tree.

After some more examination of the rash the doctor made the following declaration.

The doctor said that I had the rare tropical mango rash

and to make the rash go away, to avoid the tree.

We all had a good laugh over this "rare affliction" of the mango rash, and it became folk lore within the family.

The fear was that I had contracted some strange jungle infection, and it turned out to be nothing but skin irritation from the tree.

So this closed a chapter in the dangerous mango saga.

Every time I see a mango in the grocery store I remember this story and chuckle.
