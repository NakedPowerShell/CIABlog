Watching the Trail

Hunting for the Vietcong Highway


One of my Dad’s tasks in Laos was to assist the National Security Agency (NSA) and military intelligence with electronic monitoring of the Ho Chi Minh Trail (HCMT). The Trail was a series of long paths, trails and roads that the Communists used to supply their war effort and infiltrate. Much of the Trail was small footpaths that a single worker or enemy solider would carry what he could hundreds of miles on foot. Some of cargo was carried on bicycles, oxen, carts, and occasionally a vehicle.

 

Every year from 1967 onward, Hanoi had moved another 100,000 NVA replacements down the Trail and into South Vietnam. 

Mendez, Antonio J.. The Master of Disguise: My Secret Life in the CIA (p. 93). HarperCollins. Kindle Edition.

On the Trail, devices like this were dropped into the jungle to count larger vehicles and even elephants.

 The word trail is actually a misnomer, since the HCMT was a large network of paths, roads, and trails. The HCMT started in North Vietnam (NVN) and winded through Laos and Cambodia and into South Vietnam (SVN). In effect the Trail help supply the enemy with supplies, weapons, ammunition, and fresh troops.


This round-the-clock aerial effort was directed by "Operation Igloo White", run out of Nakhon Phanom (NKP aka Naked Fanny), Thailand.

It was composed of three parts: strings of air-dropped acoustic and seismic sensors collected intelligence on the trail; computers at the Intelligence Collection Center (ICS) in Thailand collated the information and predicted convoy paths and speeds; and an airborne relay and control aircraft which received the signals from the sensors and routed aircraft to targets as directed by the ISC.

My Father helped get some of the sensors developed and deployed from the air. He had to travel to various countries to work with vendors to build these complicated instruments based upon technology of 1968.

From this intelligence, strike missions for bombing the HCMT were planned and executed. Bases like Udorn Thailand [story] were used to damage the Trail, using B-52’s, F-4’s, and F-105’s.

More tonnage of bombs were dropped on Laos than the entire WW II in Europe.
